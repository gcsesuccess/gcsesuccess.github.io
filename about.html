GCSE Success

We aim to address socioeconomic inequality by providing free-of-charge one-to-one tuition and mentoring to advance the education and aspirations of young people. GCSE Success was established in 2012 and is supported by the Friends of York Gardens Library, GLL and Wandsworth Council.

About us

GCSE Success is a community project with tuition offered by volunteers from the local area, who also plan and run the weekly sessions. The scheme is overseen by a Management Committee comprised of tutors and non-voting advisory members.

The Management Committee

Ella Spencer is a resident of the York Road Estate and the founder of GCSE Success, which was set up in 2011. She now chairs the management committee and acts as the coordinator for GCSE schemes. Ella is very passionate about encouraging equality in education, and this is the focus of GCSE Success, as you can see in the mission statement and objectives of the organisation. Ella also recognises the importance of staying engaged in the policy debate in this area. Ella has a BSc. in mathematics and English Literature from the University of Manchester and an MPA in Economic and Public Policy from London School of Economics. She also has five years private sector experience as an actuary, working for a number of multinational companies. Ella is currently pursuing a career in development consultancy, having worked on development projects in Gambia, Ghana and South Sudan. A number of these were related to education.

James Barber is a former resident of Fulham (now living nearby in Lambeth) and has been volunteering as a GCSE Maths tutor since moving to London in September 2012. Since then, he has also taken on the responsibilities of Treasurer and Resources Manager for GCSE Success as a member of the management committee. James is a strong believer that investing in education is the key to generating prospects for individuals, and wider society, by equipping people the skills and inspiration they need to make the most of opportunities. James graduated from Oxford University in 2012 with a first class Master's degree in MEng Engineering, Economics & Management. During the week he works in the city for a top consultancy, specialising in Technology Strategy, Operating Model and Organisational Design.

Sarah Swash leads the mentoring and evaluation strategies for GCSE Success. She lives locally in Wandsworth and joined the management committee in 2013. She believes strongly that all young people should have the opportunity to succeed and contribute to society, and that individualised tuition and support can be instrumental in achieving this. Sarah has a PhD in Experimental Psychology from Oxford University and a BSc in Psychology from York.

Mark Woodbridge is the volunteer co-ordinator for GCSE Success. He is a Latchmere resident and been tutoring maths on the scheme since February 2012. Mark has a strong interest in widening access to individualised tuition and the use of technology in education. He studied Computer Science and Computational Linguistics and has worked at the University of Cambridge and Imperial College London.
